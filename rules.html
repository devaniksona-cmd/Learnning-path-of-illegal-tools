⚖ LEGAL GUIDE

The 8 Golden
Rules

The rules that keep your bug bounty hunting legal, ethical, and protected.

01
Golden Rules
1
Only test assets that are explicitly listed in the program's scope document.
2
Never cause damage or data loss — stop the moment you confirm a vulnerability exists.
3
Never access, read, copy, or store real user data under any circumstance.
4
Never use DoS / DDoS tools — ever, under any circumstance, on any target.
5
Always read the program's full rules and scope in detail before touching anything.
6
Never test without a written scope document — it is your only legal protection.
7
Always report what you find responsibly — never weaponize or sell vulnerabilities.
8
When in doubt → do not proceed. Ask the program team first.
02
Why You're Blocked at Every Level
1
GitHub / GitLab
Repos violating ToS are permanently removed. Many tools are already gone from public registries.
2
Antivirus / EDR
Files quarantined the moment they're downloaded. Windows Defender, CrowdStrike, SentinelOne all flag these.
3
ISPs
Malicious traffic patterns detected and blocked before packets even reach the target server.
4
Target Platforms
Bot detection, rate limiting, and anomaly detection ban your IP and account within seconds.
5
Law Enforcement
IP traced, ISP subpoenaed, arrest and prosecution under CFAA or equivalent laws in your country.
03
Pre-Test Checklist

Click each item to check it off before you start testing.

Is the target explicitly listed in the program scope?
Does the program allow automated scanning?
Am I using a test account — not real user data?
Have I set rate limits on all my tools?
Am I testing on a staging/dev environment (not production)?
Do I have the program's rules open in front of me?
Do I know the out-of-scope assets and will avoid them?
Am I ready to stop and report immediately if I find something?
⚖ The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA), UK Computer Misuse Act, and equivalent laws worldwide make unauthorized computer access a criminal offense — even when a real vulnerability is discovered. Your program scope document is your only legal protection.
